Why Green Teams

Communities in Africa have millions of unemployed yet educated youth and other committed community members with untapped skills and knowledge. These youth could be a huge social investment for effective mitigation of global climate challenges. There is increasing pressure to combat the global Climate Crisis through various approaches. Many approaches have failed in the past and need to be researched further to make a difference. The problem of unemployment needs to be connected to the global crisis with clear cut solutions to addressing it.

First, Financial, social and ecological pressures mean that new kind of livelihoods programmes must emerge. Secondly, these must meet the short-term imperative: engage youth on a large scale to prevent social conflict, and be cost effective and largely rely on private flows for sustainability. Thirdly, they must meet the long-term imperative: support the emergence of sustainable value chains led by the communities at the bottom of the pyramid.
There are emerging technologies around which sustainable business systems can be built. That opens up a new avenue for action, and makes the above possible. The Green Team continues to identify and develop value chains that create the jobs necessary to change the dynamics of unemployment through investing in people and thus transforming livelihoods and community level.

Climate solutions

Climate change is now affecting every country across the world. Disruptions on national economies are evident today and effects are being manifested on lives, costing people, communities and countries at an ever increasing cost.

Today people are experiencing significant impacts of climate change, ranging from changing weather patterns, rising sea level, and more extreme weather patterns. The greenhouse gas emissions from human activities are driving climate change and continue to rise.

They are now at their highest levels in history. In yet another warning from the World Meteorological Organization, the greenhouse gas levels have hit new highs in 2022 of methane concentrations since start of measurements underlining, once again, the enormous challenge – and the vital necessity – of urgent action to cut greenhouse gas emissions and prevent global temperatures rising even further in the future. Without action, the world’s average surface temperature is projected to rise over the 21st century and is likely to surpass 3 degrees Celsius this century—with some areas of the world expected to warm even more. The poorest and most vulnerable people are being affected the most.

Affordable, scalable solutions are now available to enable countries to leapfrog to cleaner, more resilient economies. The pace of change is quickening as more people are turning to renewable energy and a range of other measures that will reduce emissions and increase adaptation efforts. But climate change is a global challenge that does not respect national borders. Emissions anywhere affect people everywhere. It is an issue that requires solutions that need to be coordinated at the international level and it requires international cooperation to help developing countries move toward a low-carbon economy. It also requires committed individuals at community level to play their part in ensuring all their practices do not contribute to further exacerbating the situation. The Green Teams Initiative is leading that process from the bottom up.
